# Más Bandwidth — Open Ledger

This is the public, month-by-month accounting of [Más Bandwidth](https://mas-bandwidth.com)'s
open source work and the support that funds it. Every entry shows what the work
*was*, what it *cost*, and where the money *went* — including the AI-collaboration
token spend, down to the line.

**The whole point:** you're not supporting a black box. You can watch exactly
what your support does. If a number in here can't be clicked through and
verified, it's marked as an estimate. If the ledger ever stops matching
reality, that's a broken promise you can see — and you can leave.

The articles stay free. The libraries stay MIT. This is just the books, kept
in the open.

## How to read it

- Each month is one file in [`ledgers/`](ledgers/), same eight sections every
  time. The git history of this repo is itself part of the audit trail — every
  edit is a timestamped public commit, so a ledger can't be quietly rewritten
  after the fact.
- **Ledger #000** is the genesis entry, published *before* the Patreon opened:
  no support received yet, no revenue to report — but the real OSS work already
  underway, shown so you can see what there is to fund before spending a cent.
- The format lives in [`TEMPLATE.md`](TEMPLATE.md).

## The rules I hold myself to

- **Same date every month.** A late ledger reads like a massaged one.
- **Publish even a bad month.** Low support, a mistake, a slipped article — the
  bad months are when transparency actually means something.
- **Everything links out.** Commits, releases, articles — verify any line.
- **Public even to non-patrons.** Patrons fund the work; the whole world can
  read the books.
- **The privacy floor protects other people, never a bad month.** Client work
  is anonymized, supporter identities are private by default, the AI
  collaborator's raw interior stays private (you see what the tokens *bought*,
  not a transcript) — and any omission is itself disclosed. The floor is never
  a place to hide.
